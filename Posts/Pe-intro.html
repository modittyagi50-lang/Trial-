Beginner's Guide to Private Equity

Published Jul 1, 2025 • by Modit Tyagi

What is private equity?

Private equity (PE) is investment into non-public companies or buyouts of public companies that result in a delisting. PE firms raise funds from institutional investors and use those funds to acquire companies, improve performance, and sell later for a profit.

Key elements

  • Deal sourcing: Finding attractive companies.
  • Due diligence: Financial, commercial and legal checks.
  • Structuring: Use of equity + debt (LBO).
  • Value creation: Operational improvements, growth, financial engineering.

How analysts add value

Analysts build models (DCF, LBO), support due diligence and prepare investment memoranda. The skillset: Excel modelling, accounting understanding, and clear written communication.
